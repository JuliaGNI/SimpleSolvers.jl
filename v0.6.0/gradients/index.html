Gradients

The supertype Gradient comprises different ways of taking gradients:

  • GradientFunction,
  • GradientAutodiff,
  • GradientFiniteDifferences.

We first start by showing GradientAutodiff:

f(x::AbstractArray) = sum(x .^ 2)
x = rand(3)
grad = GradientAutodiff(f, x)
GradientAutodiff{Float64, typeof(Main.f), ForwardDiff.GradientConfig{ForwardDiff.Tag{typeof(Main.f), Float64}, Float64, 3, Vector{ForwardDiff.Dual{ForwardDiff.Tag{typeof(Main.f), Float64}, Float64, 3}}}}(Main.f, ForwardDiff.GradientConfig{ForwardDiff.Tag{typeof(Main.f), Float64}, Float64, 3, Vector{ForwardDiff.Dual{ForwardDiff.Tag{typeof(Main.f), Float64}, Float64, 3}}}((Partials(1.0, 0.0, 0.0), Partials(0.0, 1.0, 0.0), Partials(0.0, 0.0, 1.0)), ForwardDiff.Dual{ForwardDiff.Tag{typeof(Main.f), Float64}, Float64, 3}[Dual{ForwardDiff.Tag{typeof(Main.f), Float64}}(5.0e-324,2.0e-323,6.90550950195255e-310,6.90550950195413e-310), Dual{ForwardDiff.Tag{typeof(Main.f), Float64}}(2.5e-323,2.5e-323,6.9055095019557e-310,6.9055095019573e-310), Dual{ForwardDiff.Tag{typeof(Main.f), Float64}}(3.0e-323,4.4e-323,6.90550950195887e-310,6.90550950196045e-310)]))
